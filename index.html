The 7th National Conference on Communications (NCC) 2022

The College of Engineering, Design, Art and Technology (CEDAT), Makerere University, in collaboration with Uganda Communications Commission (UCC), is organizing the 7th National Conference on Communications (NCC 2022).

The conference will take place for two days from June 21 to June 22, 2022 in a blended approach where stakeholders can attend both physically at Makerere University and virtually via zoom. The main objective of this annual conference is to present peer-reviewed research work, discuss engineering solutions to local challenges, understand industry trends and needs.

The theme of this year's conference is "Inclusive Digital Transformation Through Innovation". Under this, two innovation competitions have been organized:
1. School ICT Club Competition 
2. Cybersecurity Competition

We invite all stakeholders in their different capacities to attend the conference so as to exchange knowledge aimed towards solving existing challenges.

School ICT Club Competition

Regional innovation competitions between school ICT clubs have been organized as part of the preparations for the NCC 2022 where regional winners will compete at the conference to determine the national winner.

The regional events were scheduled as follows:
● Western: 30 May 2022 at Hive Colab, 4th Floor - Amazon Building, Mbarara City. The competing schools were Ntare School, Mbarara High School, St. Joseph’s Vocational School, and Bubangizi Secondary School. Ntare School's project titled "Up Farm" emerged victorious.

● Central: 1 June 2022 at the CEDAT Conference Hall, Makerere University. The competing schools were Trinity College Nabbingo, Namilyango College, St. Mary's College Kisubi, Kalinabiri SS, Kansanga Seed SS, and St. Henry's College Kitovu. St. Mary's College Kisubi's project titled "School Eye" emerged victorious.

● Northern: 6 June 2022 at Kolping Hotel, Masindi. The competing schools were Masindi SS, Arua SS, Dr. Obote College and St. Gracious SS, Lira. St. Gracious SS, Lira's project titled "Green Smart Farm" emerged victorious.

● Eastern: 8 June 2022 at Paradise on the Nile Hotel, Jinja. The competing schools were Kongunga High School, Busoga College Mwiri, Iganga SS and Tororo Girls School. Tororo Girls School's project titled "Tower TV" emerged victorious.

The four winners of the regional ICT innovation competitions will automatically participate in the national competition on June 22, 2022. However, two extra schools i.e. Henry's College Kitovu and Iganga SS have been selected to join the national competition given the high potential of their projects.
